Vision

The Centre for Policy Research and Law Implementation (CPRLI) is a leading research center dedicated to conducting cutting-edge research in Public Policy and law implementation. We are a multidisciplinary team of social scientists, engineers, and researchers who are committed to making a positive impact on the world through our research. Our research is focused on solving some of the most pressing challenges in social, economic, legal and public policy areas our nation is facing today. We use a variety of cutting-edge techniques and technologies to conduct our research, and we collaborate with leading experts from around the world. We are also committed to training the next generation of social scientists and researchers. We offer a variety of vocational and training programs and opportunities for students and early-career researchers.

Objectives of CPRLI

Objectives of CPRLI (Center for Policy Research and Law Implementation)

  • To conduct research that informs and improves public policy and law. This could include research on a variety of topics, such as the impact of different policies on social and economic outcomes, the effectiveness of different legal frameworks, and the best practices for implementing public policy

  • To conduct research on the impact of different policies on social and economic outcomes. This could include empirical research on the policy execution and implementation and its responses at ground level
  • To translate research into actionable recommendations for policymakers and practitioners. This could involve developing policy briefs, white papers, and other resources that explain the findings of research and make recommendations for how policymakers can improve public policy
  • To build capacity for public policy and law research within government, academia, and the non-profit sector. This could involve providing training and technical assistance to researchers, policymakers, and practitioners, and developing tools and resources that can be used to conduct public policy and law research
  • To promote public understanding of public policy and law. This could involve developing educational materials, conducting public outreach events, and working with the media to communicate the findings of research to the public
  • To develop policy briefs and white papers that explains the findings of research and makes recommendations for how policymakers can improve public policy
  • To create an online database of public policy research, various Policies, Acts, Guidelines, Regulations, Researches etc. which can useful and accessible for policy feedback

  • Promote public understanding of public policy and law through educational materials, public outreach events, and working with the media
  • To research on the policies related to environment, social development, Tribal affairs, Cyber Security, Data Protection and other national development issues
  • To simplify the policy documents for general and marginal societies who are not educated enough to decipher it
  • To promote awareness about Innovation, Entrepreneurship, Copy right, Trademarks, Patent, and Geographical Indication tags
  • To promote Legal Consultancy, Conflict Management through Arbitration, Conciliation and Mediation
